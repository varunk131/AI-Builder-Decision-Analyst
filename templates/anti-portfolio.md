# The Anti-Portfolio

Your record of product bets you didn't make, features you killed too early, and ideas you got wrong. After 10+ entries, run /project:anti-portfolio-analysis to find your patterns.

---

### Miss #1 — 2021 Q3
**What I dismissed:** Adding a Slack integration to our analytics product
**My reasoning at the time:** "Our users live in the dashboard. Integrations are a distraction from core product."
**What actually happened:** Competitor launched Slack integration. 3 top-10 customers switched within 6 months citing "workflow fit."
**How wrong was I?** 4
**Category:** Feature Kill
**What I should have seen:** Support tickets mentioning "I wish I didn't have to open another tab" were growing 20% MoM. I categorized them as low priority.

### Miss #2 — 2022 Q1
**What I dismissed:** Self-serve pricing tier below $29/month
**My reasoning at the time:** "Sub-$29 customers aren't worth the support cost."
**What actually happened:** Competitor launched $9/month tier. Captured SMB segment. Many grew into mid-market within 18 months — and stayed.
**How wrong was I?** 5
**Category:** Pricing
**What I should have seen:** 40% of pricing page drop-offs. I assumed "tire-kickers" instead of future customers.

### Miss #3 — 2022 Q2
**What I dismissed:** Mobile app for our B2B product
**My reasoning at the time:** "B2B analytics is a desktop use case."
**What actually happened:** Executives wanted mobile dashboards for board meetings. Competitor won 2 enterprise deals we were pursuing.
**How wrong was I?** 3
**Category:** User Need
**What I should have seen:** 3 enterprise prospects asked about mobile in sales calls. I dismissed it as "nice to have."

### Miss #4 — 2022 Q4
**What I dismissed:** API-first approach for our new product line
**My reasoning at the time:** "Let's build the UI first. APIs can come later."
**What actually happened:** Developer-led adoption became dominant GTM. We were 12 months behind on APIs.
**How wrong was I?** 5
**Category:** Technology Bet
**What I should have seen:** Developer community growth was 3x faster than traditional buyer growth. I read customer surveys instead of market signals.

### Miss #5 — 2023 Q1
**What I dismissed:** Hiring a developer advocate
**My reasoning at the time:** "We need another engineer, not a community person."
**What actually happened:** Competitor with smaller product but active community grew 4x faster.
**How wrong was I?** 4
**Category:** Hiring/Team
**What I should have seen:** Organic mentions flat while competitor's grew exponentially. I attributed it to marketing budget.

### Miss #6 — 2023 Q2
**What I dismissed:** Partner integration marketplace
**My reasoning at the time:** "Focus on the 5 integrations that matter most."
**What actually happened:** Customers evaluated us on "number of integrations" as proxy for ecosystem health.
**How wrong was I?** 3
**Category:** Platform/Partnership
**What I should have seen:** Enterprise RFPs consistently asked integration count. I kept saying "quality over quantity."

### Miss #7 — 2023 Q3
**What I dismissed:** AI/ML-powered anomaly detection
**My reasoning at the time:** "AI is hype. Customers want accurate data, not predictions."
**What actually happened:** By 2024 every competitor had AI insights. We scrambled 12 months late.
**How wrong was I?** 5
**Category:** Technology Bet
**What I should have seen:** "I don't have time to stare at dashboards" = need for automated insights, not better dashboards.

### Miss #8 — 2023 Q4
**What I dismissed:** Vertical-specific templates
**My reasoning at the time:** "We're a horizontal platform. Verticalizing limits TAM."
**What actually happened:** Vertical competitors won segments we owned.
**How wrong was I?** 4
**Category:** Market Timing
**What I should have seen:** Win-rate declining in healthcare and e-commerce specifically. I looked at aggregate rates.

### Miss #9 — 2024 Q1
**What I dismissed:** User-generated template sharing between customers
**My reasoning at the time:** "Our customers are competitors. They won't share."
**What actually happened:** Competitor's template gallery became their #1 organic traffic source.
**How wrong was I?** 4
**Category:** User Need
**What I should have seen:** CS team was already manually sharing example dashboards between customers.

### Miss #10 — 2024 Q2
**What I dismissed:** Aggressive competitive pricing after new entrant raised $50M
**My reasoning at the time:** "We compete on value, not price."
**What actually happened:** New entrant captured 30% of our pipeline in two quarters. We eventually matched pricing AND offered migration credits.
**How wrong was I?** 4
**Category:** Competitive Response
**What I should have seen:** Their pricing was strategic (VC-subsidized growth), not desperate. "Value" defense only works when competitor product is meaningfully worse.
