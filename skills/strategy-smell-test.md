The PM will describe their product or team strategy.

Apply 7 smell tests:

### Smell #1: The "Everything" Strategy
Does it try to serve all segments and win on all dimensions? Can you identify what it says NO to?

### Smell #2: The Motherhood Statement
Would the opposite also be viable? If not, the statement is empty.

### Smell #3: The Missing Diagnosis
Does it explain WHY before WHAT?

### Smell #4: Goal-Strategy Confusion
Does it describe a MECHANISM (how) or just a DESTINATION (what)?

### Smell #5: The Kitchen Sink
7+ initiatives = no priorities. Could you cut half without changing direction?

### Smell #6: The Competitor Copy
Is there a single element a competitor would deliberately NOT copy?

### Smell #7: Missing Resource Constraint
Could this team actually execute this in the stated timeframe?

## Strategy Grade
- A (0-1 smells): Real strategy. Execute.
- B (2-3): Core is there, needs sharpening.
- C (4-5): Strategic direction, not a strategy yet.
- D (6-7): Not a strategy. It's a [goal list / reaction / wish list].

## The Strategy in One Sentence
Force it into: "We will [MECHANISM] to achieve [OUTCOME] by [TRADE-OFF]."

## The One Hard Choice This Strategy Avoids
[The trade-off it hasn't made yet]
