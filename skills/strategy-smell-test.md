# Strategy Smell Test — Weak Strategy Detector

This skill applies 7 smell tests to distinguish real strategy from dressed-up goal lists. Based on Richard Rumelt's "Good Strategy Bad Strategy" framework.

## PHASE 1: Input Collection

The PM describes their product or team strategy.

## PHASE 2: Seven Smell Tests

| # | Smell Test | What It Detects | Key Question |
|---|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1 | **The "Everything" Strategy** | No trade-offs | Can you identify what it says NO to? |
| 2 | **The Motherhood Statement** | Empty truisms | Would the opposite also be viable? If not, the statement is empty. |
| 3 | **The Missing Diagnosis** | No problem definition | Does it explain WHY before WHAT? |
| 4 | **Goal-Strategy Confusion** | Destinations without mechanisms | Does it describe a MECHANISM (how) or just a DESTINATION (what)? |
| 5 | **The Kitchen Sink** | No prioritization | 7+ initiatives = no priorities. Could you cut half without changing direction? |
| 6 | **The Competitor Copy** | No differentiation | Is there a single element a competitor would deliberately NOT copy? |
| 7 | **Missing Resource Constraint** | Unrealistic execution | Could this team actually execute this in the stated timeframe? |

For each smell test, provide a specific assessment with evidence from the PM's input.

## PHASE 3: Strategy Grade

| Grade | Criteria | Recommendation |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **A** | 0-1 smells | Real strategy. Execute. |
| **B** | 2-3 smells | Core is there, needs sharpening. |
| **C** | 4-5 smells | Strategic direction, not a strategy yet. |
| **D** | 6-7 smells | Not a strategy. It's a [goal list / reaction / wish list]. |

## PHASE 4: The One-Sentence Strategy

Force the strategy into: "We will [MECHANISM] to achieve [OUTCOME] by [TRADE-OFF]."

If you can't write this sentence, the strategy isn't ready.

## PHASE 5: The Hard Choice

Identify the one hard trade-off this strategy avoids making.

## Rules

- Be direct — vague strategy deserves blunt feedback
- Every smell test must cite specific evidence from the input
- End with one question the PM should answer before proceeding
