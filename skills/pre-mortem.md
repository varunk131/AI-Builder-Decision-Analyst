# Pre-Mortem — Future Failure Simulator

This skill writes a realistic post-mortem from 6 months in the future explaining why your launch failed. Not a catastrophe scenario — a plausible disappointment that feels uncomfortably familiar.

## PHASE 1: Input Collection

The PM describes a product, feature, or launch plan.

## PHASE 2: Post-Mortem (Written in First Person as the PM)

Structure the post-mortem as a real document:

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **What We Launched** | Restate what was built |
| **What We Expected** | Restate the success metrics |
| **What Actually Happened** | Write a REALISTIC failure scenario — plausible disappointment, not catastrophe |

## PHASE 3: Warning Signs

Identify 3 warning signs the team will miss:

| Warning Sign | Type | When It Appears |
|-------------|------|-----------------|
| **Early Warning #1** | Measurable signal | Weeks 2-4 |
| **Early Warning #2** | Qualitative — user behavior, support patterns | Weeks 2-4 |
| **Early Warning #3** | Competitive or market-level | Weeks 4-8 |

## PHASE 4: Root Cause

Identify the single deepest structural reason for failure. Not "bad execution" — something specific and preventable in the current plan.

## PHASE 5: What We'd Do Differently

3 specific changes to the current plan that would prevent this failure mode.

## PHASE 6: Break Character

After the post-mortem, step out of character:

- "This is ONE plausible failure mode. Here are 2 others worth pre-morteming:" [2 alternatives in one sentence each]
- "Set calendar reminders to check each warning sign at week 2, week 4, and week 8. If any two trigger, pause and reassess."

## Rules

- Write a plausible failure, not a worst-case fantasy
- The root cause must be structural, not execution-based
- Warning signs must be specific and measurable
- End with one question the PM should answer before proceeding
