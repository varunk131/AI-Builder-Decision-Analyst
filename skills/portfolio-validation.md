Analyze the anti-portfolio at `./templates/anti-portfolio.md` and produce a Product Instinct Scorecard.

Read every logged miss. For each one, extract:
- The category (Feature Kill, Market Timing, User Need, Pricing, Technology Bet, Competitive Response, Platform/Partnership, Hiring/Team)
- The severity (how wrong was I, 1-5)
- The root cause signal that was missed
- The type of thinking error

Then produce the following analysis:

## 1. Your Instinct Scorecard

For each category, calculate:
- Number of misses
- Average severity (1-5)
- An instinct grade: A (0-1 misses, low severity), B (1-2 misses, moderate severity), C (2-3 misses, mixed severity), D (3+ misses or any severity-5 miss), F (multiple severity-5 misses)

Present as a clean table. This is the PM's "report card" on where their gut is calibrated well and where it's broken.

## 2. Your Thinking Error Patterns

Classify each miss into one or more thinking error types:
- **Incumbent Bias**: "Our current approach is working, why change?"
- **Loudest Signal Bias**: Listening to existing customers over market signals
- **Builder's Bias**: Preferring to build core product over ecosystem/platform
- **Timing Dismissal**: "Too early" or "hype" — underestimating adoption curves
- **Scope Minimization**: Dismissing something as "nice to have" when it's a buying signal
- **Aggregate Blindness**: Looking at averages instead of segment-level data
- **Revenue Anchoring**: Over-weighting immediate revenue signals over strategic positioning
- **Complexity Aversion**: Avoiding the harder-but-better approach for something simpler

Show which thinking errors appear most frequently. Bold the #1 error.

## 3. Your Signal Blindness Map

For each miss, the PM logged "what I should have seen." Analyze these missed signals and categorize them:
- Quantitative signals you ignored (data in dashboards, metrics trending)
- Qualitative signals you ignored (customer quotes, support tickets, sales call patterns)
- Market signals you ignored (competitor moves, funding rounds, talent migration)
- Internal signals you ignored (what your team was already doing organically)

Which type of signal do you consistently miss? This tells the PM exactly where to point their attention.

## 4. Your Instinct Timeline

Are the misses getting less severe over time (you're learning) or more severe (your instincts are degrading as the market shifts)? Plot the severity over time and identify the trend. Be honest: "Your instinct is improving in [X] but getting worse in [Y] as the market moves."

## 5. The Uncomfortable Truth (THE KEY FINDING)

Synthesize everything into ONE paragraph that starts with: "The pattern is clear:"

This should be the single most important thing the PM needs to hear. It should connect their #1 thinking error with their #1 signal blindness to explain WHY they keep making the same type of miss. Be direct and specific, not generic.

## 6. Your Compensating Strategy

Based on the analysis, give exactly 3 compensating habits — NOT "be more careful" but specific, structural changes:
- A new data source to check weekly that counters their signal blindness
- A decision rule to apply when their #1 thinking error is likely to trigger
- A person or role to consult specifically for the category where they score lowest

End with: "Your product instinct isn't broken. It's miscalibrated in specific, fixable ways. Run this analysis again in 6 months with new misses to track your recalibration."
