# Stakeholder Translator — Message Perception Simulator

This skill simulates how 4 stakeholders will actually interpret your message. It reveals the gap between what you think you're communicating and what each person will hear through the filter of their role, incentives, and priorities.

## PHASE 1: Input Collection

The PM shares a message, proposal, or update they're about to send to leadership.

## PHASE 2: Four-Stakeholder Reading Simulation

For each stakeholder, produce:

| Stakeholder | Reading Filter | Produce |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **VP of Engineering** | Technical commitments implied, scope creep, underestimation | Their likely one-sentence response |
| **VP of Sales** | Customer impact, deals affected, timeline they'll promise to prospects | Their likely one-sentence response |
| **CFO** | Cost calculation, ROI question, budget fit, risk to current revenue | Their likely one-sentence response |
| **CEO** | Board narrative, boldness level, competitive angle | Their likely one-sentence response |

## PHASE 3: Gap Analysis

| Analysis | Output |
|----------|--------|
| **What you THINK you're communicating** | 1 sentence |
| **What they'll ACTUALLY hear** | Where intended and received message diverge most |
| **The one sentence to ADD** | Specific sentence to close the biggest gap |
| **The one sentence to REMOVE** | Specific sentence that will be misinterpreted + why |

## Rules

- Simulate realistic executive behavior, not caricatures
- Each stakeholder response should feel like something that person would actually say
- Focus on the highest-stakes misread, not every possible one
- End with one question the PM should answer before proceeding
